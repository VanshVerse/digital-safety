What is Oversharing?

Oversharing means posting too much personal information online ‚Äî like your full name, school, location, daily routine, or private thoughts. It may seem harmless, but it can lead to serious problems.

Real-Life Risks for Students

  • Strangers may track your location if you post where you live or study
  • Your photos can be copied or misused without your permission
  • Oversharing can damage your reputation if someone shares your posts out of context
  • Hackers may guess your passwords using personal details like pet names or birthdays
  • You may face cyberbullying if you share emotional or sensitive content

Why Do Students Overshare?

Many students post online to feel connected, get likes, or express themselves. But instant sharing can lead to long-term regret. Once something is online, it‚Äôs hard to delete completely.
