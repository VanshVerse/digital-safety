How Cyberbullying Happens in Real Life

Scenario 1: A student named Riya uploads a dance video on Instagram. Her classmates start posting comments like “cringe,” “you look weird,” and tag others to laugh. Riya feels embarrassed, deletes the video, and stops posting anything online.
Scenario 2: In a school WhatsApp group, someone creates a fake account using Aarav’s photo and sends rude messages. Other students believe it’s Aarav and start ignoring him. Aarav feels helpless and scared to speak up.
Scenario 3: During an online game, a player keeps targeting one student, calling them names like “loser” and “noob.” The student feels bullied and stops playing the game, even though it was their favorite.
Scenario 4: A student shares a funny meme in a group. Another student edits it to make fun of them and spreads it across other groups. The original student feels insulted and starts avoiding school friends.

What Can We Learn?

These situations may seem small, but they can deeply affect someone’s confidence and mental health. Cyberbullying is not just about jokes — it’s about how those jokes make someone feel. If you see bullying, don’t join in. Be the one who supports and speaks up.

Students should remember: online actions have offline consequences. Let’s build a respectful and safe digital environment where everyone feels included and valued.
